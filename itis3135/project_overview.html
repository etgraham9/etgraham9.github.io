Woodworking Website Project Overview

Primary Users

Individuals interested in woodworking, potential customers, DIY enthusiasts, and people looking for custom woodwork.

Secondary Users

Visitors curious about woodworking projects, people who want to learn more about woodworking techniques, or those who are exploring woodworking as a hobby.

Overview of Website Content

  • Home Page: Introduction to the website, brief info about Father’s woodworking background, and a preview of the latest projects.
  • Gallery Page: A visual gallery of the woodworking projects, categorized (e.g., furniture, cabinetry, outdoor, etc.).
  • About Page: Information about Father's journey in woodworking, his philosophy, and his approach to craftsmanship.
  • Contact Page: A form for users to reach out to inquire about commissions, ask questions, or request quotes.
  • Blog Page (Optional): Any woodworking tips, blog posts, or tutorials, this page will serve as a space for informative content.

Page Design Details

1. Home Page (index.html)

  • Purpose: Introduce the website and my father’s woodworking.
  • Audience: General visitors, potential customers, hobbyists.
  • Content:
    • Hero section with a short description of my father's woodworking passion.
    • A carousel or featured section with images of completed projects.
  • Data Entry: None
  • Validation: None
  • Actions: Visitors can navigate to other pages through the navigation menu.
  • Special Notes: Ensure this page includes a welcoming and visually appealing introduction to hook visitors.

2. Gallery Page (gallery.html)

  • Purpose: Display a collection of my father’s woodworking projects.
  • Audience: Potential customers, enthusiasts, and general visitors.
  • Content:
    • A grid or masonry layout of images from different project categories.
    • A description under each image.
  • Data Entry: None
  • Validation: None
  • Actions: Clicking an image could open a lightbox with more details about that project.
  • Special Notes: Use jQuery for the lightbox functionality.

3. About Page (about.html)

  • Purpose: Give a personal touch by sharing my father's journey and craftsmanship philosophy.
  • Audience: General visitors who want to know more about the artist behind the work.
  • Content: Text, personal story, photos of the workshop, and maybe even a video.
  • Data Entry: None
  • Validation: None
  • Actions: Visitors can navigate to other pages.
  • Special Notes: This page should have an authentic, personal feel to connect with visitors.

4. Contact Page (contact.html)

  • Purpose: Allow potential customers or interested visitors to contact my father.
  • Audience: Anyone interested in commissioning projects or asking questions.
  • Content:
    • A simple contact form (name, email, message).
    • Contact information (phone, email).
  • Data Entry: Name, email, and message fields.
  • Validation: Ensure that the email format is correct and the message field isn’t empty.
  • Actions: Form submission via JavaScript, with a confirmation or thank-you message.
  • Special Notes: Use simple JavaScript to validate the form.

5. Blog Page (blog.html)

  • Purpose: Share woodworking tips, tutorials, or project updates.
  • Audience: People interested in learning or getting inspired.
  • Content: Articles or posts about woodworking techniques or new projects.
  • Data Entry: None for now.
  • Validation: None.
  • Actions: Visitors can navigate to posts or articles.

Dynamic Functionality

  • Gallery (Interactive Images):
    • Users can click on project images to view them in a lightbox.
    • Implement with JavaScript/jQuery.
  • Contact Form with Validation:
    • Use JavaScript to validate the form fields before submission.
  • AJAX for Form Submission:
    • Submit the contact form without reloading the page, providing a smooth user experience.
